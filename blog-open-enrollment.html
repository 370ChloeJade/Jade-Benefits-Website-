Health Insurance Open Enrollment 2026: Dates, Deadlines & How to Prepare

By Jade Benefits | Fort Lauderdale, FL | Updated 2026

Open enrollment is the one time each year when almost anyone can sign up for a health plan, switch plans, or update their coverage, no questions asked. Miss it and you may be locked out until the next year unless a major life event opens a special window. This guide walks you through the key dates, what a Special Enrollment Period is, and a simple checklist so you walk in prepared instead of scrambling at the deadline.

What open enrollment actually is

Open enrollment is a set window each year when you can enroll in a health insurance plan or change the one you have without needing a qualifying reason. Outside of this window, the marketplace is generally closed to new sign ups unless you experience a life event. The window applies to individual and family plans bought through the ACA marketplace or directly; employer group plans run their own annual enrollment period set by the employer.

Key dates and deadlines

For most states that use the federal marketplace, the individual open enrollment window runs on this general schedule:

  • November 1 — Open enrollment begins. You can browse plans and enroll.
  • December 15 — Enroll by this date for coverage that starts January 1.
  • January 15 — Final deadline to enroll. Plans selected after December 15 usually start February 1.

A handful of state based exchanges set their own slightly different windows, and dates can shift year to year, so always confirm the current schedule for your state before you rely on it. Employer group plans have their own dates, which your HR department or broker will announce.

Quick tip: Do not wait for the final deadline. Enrolling early means your coverage starts January 1, gives you time to confirm your doctors are in network, and avoids the last minute rush when help is hardest to reach.

Special Enrollment Periods

If you miss open enrollment, a Special Enrollment Period, or SEP, may still let you sign up. An SEP is usually a 60 day window that opens after a qualifying life event, including:

  • Losing existing coverage, such as leaving a job or aging off a parent's plan
  • Getting married or divorced
  • Having a baby or adopting a child
  • Moving to a new area with different plan options
  • A change in income that affects your eligibility for subsidies

In addition, households with income below a certain level can often enroll year round. If you are not sure whether you qualify, it is worth a quick call, because many people assume they are locked out when they actually are not.

Your preparation checklist

Walk in ready with these five things and enrollment takes minutes instead of hours:

  • Income estimate for the coming year, since it determines your subsidy.
  • List of your doctors and medications so you can confirm they are covered.
  • Last year's plan details if you are renewing or switching.
  • Household information including everyone who needs coverage.
  • A budget for what you can comfortably pay in premiums and out of pocket costs.

Common mistakes to avoid

The biggest errors people make during open enrollment are auto renewing without checking whether their plan changed, underestimating income and owing money at tax time, or choosing the lowest premium without checking the deductible and network. A few minutes of review, or a free call with a broker, prevents all three.

Frequently asked questions

When is open enrollment for health insurance?

For most states on the federal marketplace, individual open enrollment runs from November 1 to January 15, with coverage starting January 1 if you enroll by December 15. Some state exchanges differ, so confirm your state's dates before the window closes.

What happens if I miss open enrollment?

You generally cannot buy a marketplace plan until the next window unless you qualify for a Special Enrollment Period triggered by a life event, or your income qualifies you for year round enrollment.

What is a Special Enrollment Period?

It is a window, usually 60 days, that opens after a qualifying life event such as job loss, marriage, a move, or a new baby, letting you enroll or change plans outside the normal window.

Does open enrollment cost anything?

No. Enrolling is free, and working with a licensed broker like Jade Benefits costs you nothing, since brokers are paid by the carrier, not by you.
